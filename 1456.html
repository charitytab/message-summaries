Where Is That Ripened Bride Seed Restored Back By Malachi 4

Minister: Brother Joseph Coleman
Date Preached: Sunday, June 6, 1993 AM
Location: Local Christian Assembly, Forest Hills, New York
Subject: He Shall Baptize with the Holy Ghost and with Fire

Summary

The Ripened Bride Seed restored by Malachi 4 is now manifesting in the Wheat season as the predestinated people of God are sealed with the Holy Ghost and Fire to bring forth the promised revival before the Tribulation.

Scripture Foundation

  • Main text reference: St. Luke 3:15–22 — John's declaration of Jesus and the baptism with the Holy Ghost and Fire

Also referenced: Malachi 4 (the restoring prophet); II Peter 1:4–7 (food value of the faith); St. Luke 22:31–34 (sifting as wheat); Isaiah 7:14, Isaiah 9:6 (the Word Seed); Deuteronomy 25:4, Isaiah 28:27 (grain processing)

Main Points

  • The Barley was Unbreakable, the Wheat Must Ripen. The Barley Revelation (five Barley loaves—Paul, Irenaeus, Martin, Columba, and Malachi 4) could not be tampered with or leavened. Now we are in the Wheat season, which requires natural ripening through both the former rain and latter rain to bring forth the full maturity of the Bride Seed.
  • Wheat Requires Three Primary Nutrients and Twelve Minerals. Wheat needs nitrogen, phosphorous, and potassium—corresponding to Justification, Sanctification, and the Baptism of the Holy Ghost. It also requires twelve soil minerals, representing the Apostles' Doctrine. Without all these elements, the crop fails.
  • The Dormant Period Is Essential, Not a Failure. Winter wheat must go through dormancy to mature and produce seed. The Prophet was taken away in December 1962, planting the Seed in dormancy for twenty-seven and a half years. The people must not despair during this silent season but continue to praise the Lord, knowing that life is still present beneath the ground.
  • The Farmer Signals When the Wheat Is Ripe. When the grain ripens, the farmer tests it by hand, checking for full kernels. Once ripe, he signals for swift harvest—a short, quick work to gather the wheat before the weeds (false anointed ones) can rise up in the hot summer sun and overtake the field. Brother Coleman ensured New York City would be a strong wheat field.
  • The Ripened Word Seed in Christ Introduced Messiah and the Restored Message. Jesus was thirty years old when He was the ripened Word Seed; the promised Elijah in 1963 was also ripened when seven Angels anointed him, opened the Seven Seals, and revealed every mystery in the Bible to sow a Word Bride for the rapture before the Tribulation.

Brother Coleman opens the message with the revelation that the Bride already possesses the Seven Thunder Seeds within—the foundational message and power needed for revival.

This agricultural principle illustrates why Brother Coleman made sure the Word of God went into the hearts of believers—so there would be no empty seed heads (hollow believers) in the harvest.

Quoting Luke 22:31, Brother Coleman assures believers that being sifted (tested) as wheat is evidence of God's purpose in their lives, not a sign of failure.

"Peace floods my heart. I just want to do anything I can, to help others come to this same experience."

Final Exhortation

Believers are exhorted to recognize that they are Wheat Words, predestinated from the foundation of the world and sealed with the Holy Ghost and Fire. The Wheat season has arrived—the time of ripening and swift harvest. Do not despair in trials or during silent periods; instead, travail in prayer and expectation for the Promise of Power from on high. When the anointing comes, it will teach and direct without human effort. Stand firm as a Caleb Seed, let your faith not fail, and strengthen your brethren by putting fire into the services so that the rest of the predestinated Bride may be sealed before the great Tribulation.
