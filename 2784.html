The Faith Of Abraham

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Friday, April 24, 1959 (afternoon)
Location: San Jose, California
Reference: 59-0424A

Summary

God's compound redemptive Names — Jehovah-Jireh, Jehovah-Rapha, and all the rest — are inseparable expressions of His one nature; if He is still the Lord who provides a sacrifice, He is still the Lord who heals, and the faith of Abraham that pleased God was simply taking Him at His Word regardless of every contradicting circumstance.

Scripture Foundation

  • Genesis 22:14 — "Abraham called the name of the place Jehovah-jireh" (primary text).
  • Genesis 17 — God appears to Abraham as Almighty God (El Shaddai), the continuation from the previous service.

Also referenced: Romans 4:17–21 (Abraham considered not his own body now dead); Ephesians 4:30 ("Grieve not the Holy Spirit whereby you are sealed until the day of your redemption"); Hebrews 11 (the faith chapter); Genesis 18 (Sarah and Abraham visited by the Lord before Isaac's birth).

Main Points

  • God's compound redemptive Names cannot be separated. Jehovah-Jireh (the Lord will provide a sacrifice), Jehovah-Rapha (the Lord who heals), and every other redemptive Name belong to one unchanging nature. If He is no longer Jehovah-Rapha, then He is no longer Jehovah-Jireh, and there is no sacrifice for sin — but He is both, yesterday, today, and forever.
  • Abraham was called by election, not by merit. God chose Abraham out of Ur as an ordinary man and made him a promise. In the same way, every believer is called by God's foreknowledge — "No man can come to Me except My Father draws him first" — and sealed by the Holy Spirit until the day of redemption.
  • Real faith does not consider contradicting symptoms. Abraham's body was as good as dead and Sarah's womb was barren, yet he "staggered not at the promise." Brother Branham illustrates with a woman whose ulcered stomach was pronounced healed by the Spirit; six weeks of unchanged symptoms did not cancel the Word, and one morning the healing manifested.
  • God turned Abraham and Sarah back to youth — a type of the resurrection. God did not patch them up; He renewed them entirely so Sarah could bear Isaac and nurse him. This foreshadows what He will do for all the seed of Abraham at the coming of the Lord: "Not old gray-headed and broke down, but young in the very splendor of health."
  • Jehovah-Jireh: "The Lord will provide Himself a sacrifice." When Isaac asked, "Where is the lamb?" Abraham answered, "God will provide." On that same mountain, God stayed Abraham's hand and provided a ram, pointing forward to Christ — "God's provided Sacrifice, the same yesterday, today, and forever."

Quote

"My son, God will provide a lamb for that burnt offering. Just got a few minutes to do it."

Abraham's reply to Isaac on Mount Moriah — the ultimate confession of faith: not knowing how, but knowing Who.

"I don't care how black it looks, how many prayer lines you been through… take God at His Word and step forward. He's Jehovah-Jireh, the Lord will provide for Himself a sacrifice."

Brother Branham's closing appeal, linking Abraham's faith directly to the believer's present need.

Final Exhortation

The same faith that kept Abraham climbing that mountain with a knife in one hand and his son's hand in the other is the faith God expects of every seed of Abraham today. Do not consider the symptoms, the doctor's report, or the opinions of those around you. God's Word cannot fail. Jesus Christ is the everlasting, perpetual Sacrifice — Jehovah-Jireh fulfilled — and the Spirit that raised Him from the dead now dwells in every born-again believer. Take Him at His Word and step forward.
