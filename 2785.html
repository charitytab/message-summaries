Hear Ye Him

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Friday, April 24, 1959 (evening)
Location: San Jose, California
Reference: 59-0424E

Summary

In this Message, Brother William Branham takes the text "Hear ye Him" from Matthew 17:5 to present a foundation for faith and obedience to Christ. He emphasizes that true Christianity rests not on doctrines or denominational differences, but on personal relationship with the living Christ who is the same yesterday, today, and forever. The Message centers on the power of faith expressed through simple obedience to God's Word, demonstrating Christ's authority over circumstances, disease, and death through both Scripture and testimony.

Scripture Foundation

  • Matthew 17:5 — "Hear ye Him" — The primary text, spoken at the transfiguration, establishing Christ's authority and the necessity of listening directly to His Word.

Also referenced: John 14:12, Hebrews 13:8, John 5:39-40, Acts 19:11-12, John 11:25-26

Main Points

  • The Quality of God's Word Transcends Quantity. Brother Branham uses the illustration of a rare postage stamp to show that the value of God's Word lies not in its physical length but in its eternal power. Three words—"Hear ye Him"—carry enough power to save the entire world.
  • Christ's Identity as God-man. He demonstrates that Jesus proved His divinity through works: feeding five thousand with five loaves and two fishes, commanding the sea to be still, and raising Lazarus from the dead after four days. He was man when He wept but God when He spoke resurrection life.
  • Jesus Christ Remains Unchanged. The promise of Hebrews 13:8—that Jesus is the same yesterday, today, and forever—means believers can encounter His power and presence in their own time. As He functioned as High Priest in biblical days, He functions the same way now among His people.
  • Faith Requires Personal Connection, Not Mediators. Brother Branham teaches that the world seeks Jesus through various institutional channels, but the Church lost Him at Pentecost and must return to where they left Him—to the direct presence of the Holy Spirit. Many try to find Him in their religious kinfolks rather than in intimate relationship.
  • Love as the True Motivator of Faith. True faith is motivated and sustained by God's love. Brother Branham illustrates this through the story of a mother deer sacrificing herself for her fawn, which moved a hardened hunter to salvation—a display of genuine, selfless love that transcends reason and fear.
  • Obedience and Faith Work Together. The Message teaches that faith without corresponding action is incomplete; believers must respond to God's Word with whole-hearted commitment, just as Abraham responded with absolute belief regardless of natural circumstances.

Quote

"Hear ye Him." Friends, if I could find one thing that I could direct people to, I would say these three words. He is our Saviour and our Healer.

This core statement encapsulates the Message: a simple call to listen directly to Jesus, who alone holds the power to save and to heal. All of God's promises and blessings flow from this one directive—to hear and obey His voice.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham concludes by calling believers to receive the love of God in their hearts and display it to the world with the same sacrificial devotion as the mother deer in his testimony. He invites those whose hearts are stirred by God's voice to accept Christ's love and let it motivate them to go forth in faith, no matter what the natural circumstances or the world's opinion declares. The Message ends in worship and intercession, committing all who respond to God as trophies of His Word.
