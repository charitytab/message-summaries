We Would See Jesus

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Tuesday, April 22, 1959
Location: San Jose, California
Reference: 59-0422

Summary

In this Message, Brother Branham emphasizes the urgency of seeking the living presence and power of Jesus Christ in the believer's life. He contrasts the natural senses and human reasoning with the spiritual perception required to encounter Christ, arguing that true faith comes not from observing signs alone but from a living, personal relationship with the risen Christ who operates through His Church today.

Scripture Foundation

  • John 12:21 — "Sir, we would see Jesus"—the foundational text expressing humanity's deep desire to encounter Christ personally.

Also referenced: Luke 24 (resurrection appearances), Matthew 24 (signs of the times), Revelation (end-times prophecy), Hebrews 13:8 (Jesus Christ the same yesterday, today, and forever)

Main Points

  • The Reality of Christ's Resurrection Presence. Christ promised His disciples that He would be with them always. Brother Branham emphasizes that the resurrected Christ operates through the Holy Ghost in power and demonstration today, not merely as a historical memory but as a living, present reality in the Church.
  • Signs and Wonders as Evidence of Christ's Living Presence. The healing miracles and supernatural signs that Brother Branham witnessed in recent meetings (hundred of souls, wheelchairs, cancers healed) are not isolated events but proof that the same Jesus who walked with the disciples is present and working in power through faith.
  • Human Reason Cannot Comprehend Spiritual Reality. The five senses and natural reasoning cannot perceive or verify spiritual things. Genuine faith requires a spiritual perception that transcends human intellect—believers must see with eyes that have been opened spiritually.
  • The Imminence of God's Judgment and Return. Brother Branham discusses current geopolitical tensions (atomic missiles, Russia's military power) and describes them as fulfillment of Bible prophecy regarding the end times. Just as judgment came in the days of Lot and Sodom, the world faces imminent judgment, heightening the need for repentance and faith.
  • The Appeal for Salvation and Repentance. Brother Branham calls sinners and backsliders to stand and accept Christ while the Holy Spirit's presence is evident in the meeting, warning that such opportunities may not come again.
  • The Holy Spirit's Authority Over Sickness and Demons. As the resurrected Christ operates through the Holy Ghost, the Spirit holds authority over sickness and demonic forces. Believers who yield themselves to the Spirit's power can pray for the sick and cast out demons in Jesus' Name.

Quote

"Being a believer in the Scripture, I think there's no meeting complete without the reading of the Word. The Word is God's foundation. I believe all doctrines, everything should come exactly from the Word."

Brother Branham stresses that all spiritual authority and doctrine must rest firmly on Scripture, not on human tradition or experience alone. The Word of God is the immovable foundation for faith and practice.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham exhorts believers to open their eyes spiritually and perceive the living Christ present in power today. He challenges sinners and backsliders to surrender to Christ while His presence is near, warning that the world is heading toward judgment and that now is the time to make peace with God. The resurrected Jesus—the same in power and principle as when He walked the earth—is here to heal, deliver, and transform all who believe.
