And Their Eyes Were Opened And They Knew Him

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, April 21, 1957 (evening)
Location: Jeffersonville, Indiana
Reference: 57-0421E

Summary

Brother Branham delivers an Easter Message centered on the post-resurrection appearance of Christ to the disciples on the road to Emmaus. He teaches that spiritual perception—having one's eyes opened—is the key to truly knowing and recognizing Jesus. This Message emphasizes that the resurrected Christ remains alive and active through the Holy Ghost, validating His promises and demonstrating His resurrection power through healing and deliverance in the present day.

Scripture Foundation

  • Luke 24:30-31 — The disciples' eyes were opened and they recognized Jesus in the breaking of bread, the basis for the entire Message.

Also referenced: John 14 (Christ's promise to remain with believers), Romans 8 (the Holy Ghost bearing witness), Matthew 18 (two or three gathered in His Name), Hebrews 13:8 (Jesus Christ the same yesterday, today, and forever)

Main Points

  • Easter as More Than Historical Commemoration. Brother Branham distinguishes between viewing Easter merely as a historical event and recognizing it as proof of Christ's living resurrection power. Unless believers experience the evidence of the resurrected Christ acting in power today, Easter becomes only a solemn historical memory rather than a transformative spiritual reality.
  • The World Cannot Perceive the Risen Christ. Jesus said, "A little while, and the world will see Me no more." The "world order" (the unbelieving world system) cannot recognize or perceive the resurrected Christ because spiritual perception requires faith. Only believers whose eyes have been opened spiritually can know Him.
  • Christ's Resurrection Is Proven by Continued Signs and Miracles. The Holy Ghost operates in the same power and principle as Jesus Himself, working signs and wonders as proof of Christ's resurrection. Brother Branham emphasizes that believers should expect supernatural manifestations—healing, deliverance, and divine authority—as confirmation that Jesus is risen and present.
  • The Holy Ghost: The Same Spirit Operating in the Same Power. The Holy Ghost is not a substitute for Christ but Christ Himself in Spirit form, operating with the same authority and power He exercised in His earthly ministry. This is the proof of the resurrection: the living Spirit of Christ continues to heal, deliver, and work miracles.
  • Faith and Spiritual Discernment Open the Eyes. Just as the disciples' eyes were opened on the road to Emmaus when Jesus broke bread with them, modern believers' eyes are opened through faith in Christ's present reality. This is not mere intellectual assent but a living, experiential knowledge of the risen Christ.
  • The Authority and Presence of Angels in the Midst of Believers. Based on Scripture, the Angels of God are encamped about those who fear Him. Where Christ is present and believers gather in His Name, the heavenly host is also present, reinforcing Christ's authority over all demonic and sickness forces.
  • Command and Authority Over Demons and Sickness. Brother Branham demonstrates and proclaims Christ's authority through prayer, commanding unclean spirits to depart and declaring healing power available through faith in the resurrected Christ. The power of healing has been paid for through Christ's redemption and is available to all who believe.

Quote

"But brethren, the reason I do that, because I know my Redeemer liveth. And He's the Truth. Jesus Christ is alive. He's here."

Brother Branham's conviction of Christ's present, living reality forms the foundation of all spiritual authority and power demonstrated in his ministry. This is not abstract theology but a living, personal knowledge that empowers faith and action.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham calls believers to have faith and accept the Divine healing power of Almighty God. He exhorts all who believe with their whole heart that Jesus Christ healed them to stand up, accept that healing, and give God praise. The Message concludes with an urgent call for the Church to recognize and embrace the resurrected Christ working in power through the Holy Ghost today, casting out fear and all doubt, and rising to claim the promises purchased by Christ's resurrection.
