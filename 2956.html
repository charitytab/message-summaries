The Great And Mighty Conqueror

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, April 21, 1957 (morning)
Location: Jeffersonville, Indiana
Reference: 57-0421S

Summary

This Easter morning Message contrasts worldly conquerors with the supreme conquering power of Jesus Christ. While men like Napoleon and Hitler achieved earthly victories through military force and violence, dying in disgrace or defeat, Christ conquered through humility, sacrifice, and His resurrection. At thirty-three years old, Christ conquered everything in heaven, earth, and hell not through weapons but through His willing death and victorious resurrection, proving that spiritual victory is infinitely greater than temporal conquest.

Scripture Foundation

  • Matthew 28:7 - "Go quickly, and tell his disciples that he is risen from the dead; and, behold, he goeth before you into Galilee; there shall you see him: lo, I have told you." (Primary text for the commission to go and tell of the resurrection)

Also referenced: Romans 5, references to various war victories, the First and Second World Wars

Main Points

  • Great Commissions Require Great Conquering. The commission to tell others that Jesus has risen is the greatest commission ever given to mankind because it announces the greatest conquest that has ever occurred.
  • Earthly Conquerors Achieve Only Temporary Victory. Historical conquerors like Napoleon and Hitler, despite their military successes, ultimately died in disgrace, depression, and failure. They conquered through force but lost their own souls in the process.
  • Christ's Weapons Are Not Carnal. Unlike human conquerors who used muskets, cannons, and guns, Christ used humility, willing self-sacrifice, and His shed blood to win the greatest battle ever fought.
  • Christ Conquered Everything at Calvary and the Resurrection. Jesus destroyed the power of death, hell, and sin through His death, burial, and resurrection, proving His victory through the empty tomb rather than a battlefield of ruins.
  • The Victory Is Accessible to Believers. Those who accept Christ as their risen Lord share in His victory and can live as conquerors themselves, no longer bound by sin, sickness, or death.
  • Resurrection Victory Produces Eternal Joy. Unlike worldly victories that fade and disappoint, the resurrection victory of Christ provides a joy and peace that endures eternally, calling believers to stand boldly for the Gospel without shame.

Quote

"When the victory was won. The boys were coming home. It was all over...But to the Christian this morning, the victory is won. The joy bells are ringing. The war is over, between God and man. The victory's been won."

Brother Branham illustrates the difference between temporary earthly victories and Christ's eternal victory by comparing the celebration at the end of World War II with the Christian's permanent victory through Christ's resurrection.

Final Exhortation

Do not be ashamed of the Gospel in this dark hour. Christ is alive and has conquered everything that opposes Him. Stand boldly as a forward Christian, giving your testimony without timidity, knowing that the battle is finished and the victory is eternally won through His resurrection. Accept Him fully this Easter morning and live as a true conqueror in Him.
