The Entombment

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Saturday, April 20, 1957 (evening)
Location: Jeffersonville, Indiana
Reference: 57-0420

Summary

This Message centers on the unchanging faithfulness of God's Word, illustrated through biblical figures who anchored their faith in God's promises even when circumstances appeared impossible. As the central point in Easter observance—the entombment—the Message teaches that while Christ's body lay in the tomb, His soul continued the work of redemption, and this historical fact proves that every word God has spoken will be fulfilled with absolute certainty.

Scripture Foundation

  • Acts 2:25-27 - "David...I foresaw the Lord always before my face...my flesh shall rest in hope: Because thou will not leave my soul in hell, neither will thou suffer thy Holy One to see corruption." (Primary text confirming Christ's entombment and resurrection promise)

Also referenced: Genesis, Job, Abraham's covenant, Jacob's blessing, Matthew 22:43-46, Revelation 5

Main Points

  • God's Word Is Infallible and Immutable. Everything God speaks becomes truth and cannot be recalled or revised. His Word always comes to pass exactly as spoken, regardless of how circumstances appear or how much time passes.
  • Faith Rests Upon God's Eternal Word, Not Shifting Circumstances. True believers like Noah, Job, and Abraham learned to stand firm on God's promises without wavering, even when all evidence seemed contrary to what God had said.
  • God's Word Was Spoken Before the Foundation of the World. Christ's death and resurrection were not afterthoughts or surprises to God; they were determined and declared before creation itself, making them absolute certainties.
  • The Church Is Built on Divine Revelation, Not Organization. True faith in Christ comes through spiritual revelation from God, not through church membership, denomination, or human doctrine. This revelation must be personally encountered and known.
  • The Entombment Was Not Silence—Christ's Work Continued. Even though Christ's body lay in the tomb, His soul went into the realm of the departed to complete the redemptive work, conquering every region and fulfilling all Old Testament types and promises.
  • Accepting Christ as Personal Saviour Is the Critical Decision. This Message calls sinners and backsliders to accept Christ through the shed blood, not through church membership or good works, making a personal decision for spiritual resurrection.

Quote

"I know my Redeemer liveth, and at the last days He'll stand on the earth. Though the skin worms destroys this body, yet in my flesh shall I see God."

Brother Branham quotes Job's declaration of faith, showing how even in the Old Testament, believers anchored their certainty on God's promise of resurrection, a confidence based purely on God's Word without any visible evidence.

Final Exhortation

Do not delay in accepting Christ as your personal Saviour. Remember that your soul does not cease to exist when death comes; it will be in the presence of either Christ or judgment. Just as this may be your last sermon, let it be the occasion where you make peace with God. Accept the all-sufficient sacrifice of Christ's blood, abandon religious works and denominational dependency, and receive the spiritual revelation that only God's Spirit can give. Come to the altar and make your public confession of faith so that you may experience spiritual resurrection with Him.
