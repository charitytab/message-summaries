Jesus Christ The Same Yesterday, Today, And Forever

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Thursday, April 26, 1956
Location: Charlotte, North Carolina
Reference: 56-0426

Summary

Brother Branham emphasizes that the risen Christ continues to operate through the same divine gifts and supernatural power He demonstrated during His earthly ministry. The Message centers on the principle that believers purchased by Christ's blood have greater value than the blood itself, and demonstrates how the Holy Spirit still reveals hidden knowledge and brings healing to those who exercise faith in Jesus' resurrection.

Scripture Foundation

  • Hebrews 13:8 — "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and today, and forever" — the foundational text establishing Christ's unchanging nature and power across all ages.

Also referenced: John 3 (the woman at the well); Mark 16:17-18 (signs following believers); Exodus 33 (God's presence and character)

Main Points

  • The Price of Redemption. Christ values the redeemed (His purchase) more than His own blood, having given His life to redeem humanity. Believers should approach His presence with reverence and love for one another as purchased children of God.
  • Unity Beyond Sectarianism. Denominational divisions and boundary lines contradict Christ's command for believers to be brothers and sisters. The absence of true unity in the Church is a barrier to the Gospel's full impact.
  • The Resurrection Principle. Christ's promise, "If I be lifted up, I will draw all men unto Me," establishes that a living, resurrected Jesus still operates through His Church with the same power and gifts demonstrated in Matthew 28:19-20.
  • Divine Discernment and Healing. Through faith and yielded vessels, the Holy Spirit continues to reveal the hidden conditions of hearts and brings healing to the sick, replicating Christ's encounter with the woman at the well and His healing miracles.
  • Faith as the Channel. Believers do not receive healing through the minister's power but through their own faith in the resurrected Christ. The gift of healing is evidence that Christ remains actively present.
  • Response to the Holy Spirit. Immediate, believing response to God's call is essential; lingering or doubting prevents the flow of God's power. The Holy Spirit's operation requires the audience's cooperation and belief.

Quote

"Now, may the Lord grant it, and all of you promised you would believe and expressed it. May the Lord grant it as I pass to the woman."

Brother Branham begins the prayer card ministry, inviting the congregation to believe in Christ's resurrection power and to witness the same supernatural revelation He provided to the woman at the well centuries before.

Final Exhortation

Receive the risen Christ not merely as a historical figure, but as the living God actively present among His people today. Exercise faith in His unchanging power to heal, to reveal, and to transform. Unite as believers across all denominational lines to honor the blood Christ shed for your redemption, and respond immediately when the Holy Spirit moves to demonstrate His presence and power in your midst.
