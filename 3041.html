Show Us The Father And It'll Satisfy

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, April 22, 1956
Location: Spindale, North Carolina
Reference: 56-0422

Summary

Brother Branham unpacks Philip's request to Jesus—"Show us the Father, and it'll suffice us"—revealing that knowing God fully is the ultimate satisfaction of the human soul. The Message contrasts intellectual reasoning with spiritual faith, demonstrates how Christ is the perfect revelation of the Father, and urges believers to cast down carnal reasoning and trust in the supernatural power and nature of God.

Scripture Foundation

  • John 14:5-11 — Philip's plea "Show us the Father" and Jesus' response "He that hath seen Me hath seen the Father," establishing that Christ is God incarnate and the complete revelation of the Father to humanity.

Also referenced: John 11 (resurrection of Lazarus); Romans 12:2 (casting down reasoning); Matthew 14 (feeding the five thousand, walking on water); Psalm 6 (the nature of the soul and death)

Main Points

  • God Operates by Laws. Just as natural forces (gravity, electricity) operate by fixed laws, so God works by spiritual laws. A sick person cannot heal by merely calling out to God; they must understand and work according to God's laws of faith and belief.
  • Christ as the Father's Representation. Jesus is the perfect and only revelation of the Father. To have seen Christ is to have seen God; all the Father's works and nature are fully displayed in the Son, making further revelation unnecessary.
  • The Soul Versus Intellect. The human soul—the innermost spirit—knows God's truth by nature and conscience; reasoning and intellect often contradict this inner knowing. When the soul whispers "that's wrong," believers should heed it rather than rationalize away conviction.
  • Faith Over Scientific Reasoning. God cannot be known through science or intellectual proof; He is known by faith. The virgin birth, resurrection, and supernatural works cannot be validated by human logic but require faith in God's Word.
  • Christ's Dual Nature Affirmed. Jesus was fully Man (hungering, tired, weeping) yet fully Divine (controlling nature, raising the dead, feeding multitudes). This God-Man nature is the sign that proves both His humanity and His divinity.
  • Moral Decline Through Reasoning. When believers rationalize away their soul's conviction by comparing themselves to others ("If they do it, why can't I?"), they progressively fall into compromise and separation from God, fulfilling the proverb that "the soul that sinneth shall die."

Quote

"He that hath seen Me hath seen the Father: and how sayest thou then, Shew us the Father?"

Jesus' direct answer to Philip encapsulates the Message's central truth: Christ is not merely a prophet pointing to the Father, but the Father Himself incarnate, and all the power and nature of God are fully revealed in Him.

Final Exhortation

Stop seeking further revelation; Jesus Christ is the complete and final showing of the Father. Trust your soul's inner witness to God's truth rather than allowing intellectual reasoning to rationalize away conviction. When you feel in your deepest being that something is wrong, obey that inner knowing instead of defending it by comparison to what others do. Work according to God's spiritual laws by believing in your heart, and you will receive whatever you ask. The Father's nature, power, and love are fully displayed in Christ—seek Him, know Him, and let that knowledge fully satisfy your longing soul.
