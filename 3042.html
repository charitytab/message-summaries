What Visions Are

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Saturday, April 21, 1956
Location: Spindale, North Carolina
Reference: 56-0421

Summary

In this Message, Brother Branham explains that visions are God's supernatural way of confirming His Word and revealing His will. Through powerful personal testimonies—particularly an extraordinary account of a vision given two years prior that precisely foretold a tragic accident in Finland involving two children—he demonstrates that God's visions operate beyond human faith and comprehension, providing absolute proof of God's presence and power. The Message emphasizes that visions authenticated through fulfillment prove the Gospel's living power far beyond what dead religion or historical Christianity can offer.

Scripture Foundation

  • Acts 2:29 (Handkerchiefs and Aprons): The practice of praying over handkerchiefs is rooted in Scripture where believers' cloths were anointed and brought healing.

Also referenced: The nature of visions throughout Scripture demonstrating God's communication with His people; discussion of Gospel power versus the limitations of social gospel and creeds.

Main Points

  • Visions are God's Confirmation of His Word. Brother Branham opens by defending the scriptural practice of praying over handkerchiefs, establishing that biblical signs and wonders remain valid when God's Word is preached in power. Visions represent God's supernatural endorsement of His truth.
  • The Finland Vision: A Divine Prophecy. Two years before the meeting in Finland, Brother Branham saw a vision of two children being struck by a car on a mountain road with evergreen trees and rock formations. Every detail—the setting, the children's appearance, their injuries—matched perfectly when the accident occurred, proving the vision's divine origin and its fulfillment outside any human effort.
  • The Healing of the Little Girl. One of the injured children, a girl with leg braces from a war injury, was brought to the service after the accident. Brother Branham had seen her healing in the vision and called her to the platform where she was instantly healed, throwing down her braces and running freely—demonstrating God's power beyond natural medicine.
  • Visions Transcend Human Faith. Brother Branham emphasizes that visions operate beyond faith—they are the spoken Word of God in manifestation. The fulfillment of the Finland vision proved so absolute that even Russian communist soldiers, witnessing the dead child restored to life, wept and embraced their Finnish adversaries, recognizing divine power that transcends politics and worldly conflict.
  • The Living Gospel vs. Historical Religion. The Message contrasts the reality of an active, living Christ—present through the baptism of the Holy Ghost and fire—with the emptiness of social gospel, creeds, and historical accounts. A freezing man cannot be warmed by a painted picture of fire; he needs the reality. The world needs not the memory of Christ but the resurrection power of the living Christ.
  • God's Faithfulness to His People. Through the testimony of the Finnish war orphan girl and the desperate mother seeking prayer for her dying son, Brother Branham illustrates God's compassion and willingness to intervene in the suffering of His children, showing that genuine faith in God's Word produces supernatural results.

Quote

"What good would it do to show a man that's freezing to death, a big bonfire painted? That's what history does. It paints a fire. A freezing man can't get warm by a painted picture. He's got to have the reality."

This quote captures the Message's central truth: the Gospel must be a living, demonstrated reality of God's power—not merely an intellectual or historical doctrine. The world needs the actual presence and power of the resurrected Christ through the Holy Ghost, not just knowledge of what He did in the past.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham calls believers to understand that visions and supernatural manifestations are not superstition but the highest expression of God's faithfulness to His Word. He exhorts the church to seek the living demonstration of Christ's resurrection power and to recognize that God still speaks, still confirms His Word, and still works miracles for those who believe. The reality of God's Word—demonstrated through visions and healings—proves that Christ is alive and present with His people, calling forth a living faith that produces eternal results.
