When Their Eyes Were Opened

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Friday, April 20, 1956
Location: Spindale, North Carolina
Reference: 56-0420

Summary

Drawing from the account of the disciples on the Road to Emmaus in Luke 24, Brother Branham teaches that believers often fail to recognize Christ's living presence and work in their daily lives. The Message powerfully illustrates how Christ walks with us through our trials, blessings, and deliverance—yet our spiritual blindness prevents us from knowing it is Him. Through divine revelation and faith in God's Word, our eyes are opened to perceive the resurrected Christ actively sustaining and caring for us in every circumstance.

Scripture Foundation

  • Luke 24:13-35 (Road to Emmaus): The primary text describing how the risen Christ, unrecognized by the disciples, walked with them, explained the scriptures concerning Himself, and was revealed to them in the breaking of bread—a pattern showing how Christ often works incognito in our lives.

Also referenced: Hebrews 13:2 (entertaining angels unaware); The healing of the woman with the issue of blood (Mark 5) demonstrating Jesus' vision and discernment; various personal testimonies of divine deliverance and protection.

Main Points

  • Christ's Hidden Presence in Our Lives. As the disciples journeyed to Emmaus, the risen Christ walked with them yet remained unrecognized until the moment of spiritual revelation. Similarly, believers often experience Christ's presence and intervention without perceiving it is Him—in healing, deliverance, provision, and miraculous protection.
  • Conversation Reveals the Heart. Brother Branham explains that as the disciples spoke about Christ and His finished work, He drew near to them. When our focus and conversation center upon Christ, His character, and His goodness rather than earthly concerns, we become more aware of His actual presence and operation in our affairs.
  • Recognition Comes Through Scripture. The disciples' eyes were opened when Christ explained the scriptures concerning Himself, showing that spiritual understanding comes through the Word of God. Only when believers study and meditate upon God's Word do their spiritual eyes open to perceive divine reality in their circumstances.
  • Examples of Christ's Unrecognized Presence. Brother Branham provides practical illustrations: the doctor turned you down but you recovered—that was Christ. A car accident you barely avoided—Christ was at your side. The barrel of flour that never emptied during depression, children clothed and fed when provision seemed impossible—these were all instances of Christ's active care, often unperceived.
  • Divine Discernment Reveals Truth. Jesus knew when the woman touched His garment because He saw through vision what was in her heart. This divine discernment—seeing spiritually rather than naturally—enables believers and ministers to perceive spiritual realities beyond natural sight and to minister God's healing power effectively.
  • The Bread Breaking: Communion with Christ. The disciples recognized Jesus in the breaking of bread, symbolizing intimate communion with the risen Christ through faith and the Word. True recognition of Christ comes through fellowship with Him in the Spirit and in His truth.

Quote

"If you let your conversations be upon Christ, and upon His finished works, and His goodness, instead of so much stuff that we do think about all day long, Christ would appear more often to us, if we'd only look at it in that way."

This quote emphasizes that spiritual perception is directly related to where believers fix their attention and conversation. Directing our thoughts toward Christ's character, redemptive work, and tender care opens our spiritual eyes to recognize His constant presence and provision in our lives.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham exhorts believers to open their eyes to recognize Christ's constant presence in their lives. He is not absent or distant but walks beside you through every circumstance—your sorrows, your deliverances, your provisions, and your protections. Ask the Holy Spirit to give you spiritual sight to perceive these divine interventions and to recognize that the One loving you, sustaining you, and working on your behalf is none other than the resurrected Jesus Christ. Let your conversation and thoughts dwell upon His finished work and His goodness, so that your eyes might be opened to the reality of His living presence with you always.
