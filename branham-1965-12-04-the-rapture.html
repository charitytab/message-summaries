The Rapture

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Saturday, December 4, 1965
Location: Yuma, Arizona
Subject: The Rapture
Reference: 65-1204

Summary

The Rapture occurs in three sequential events—a Shout (the Message gathering the Bride), a Voice (the resurrection of the dead in Christ), and a Trump (the Bride called to the Supper in the heavens)—fulfilling 1 Thessalonians 4:16 before Jesus appears.

Scripture Foundation

  • Main text reference — 1 Thessalonians 4:13–18 (the order of the Rapture and the three-fold process: Shout, Voice, Trump)

Also referenced: Psalms 27:1–5; Revelation 3:20; Luke 17; Malachi 4; Genesis 18; Matthew 24; Revelation 4–5 (Seven Church Ages and Seven Seals)

Main Points

  • The Three-Fold Process of the Rapture. Jesus descends from heaven with (1) a Shout—the Message going forth to gather the Bride; (2) a Voice—the resurrection power that called Lazarus from the grave, raising the dead in Christ; and (3) a Trump—the final call to the Bride's Supper in the sky, fulfilling the Feast of Trumpets.
  • The Shout: Restoration of the Message. God's unchanging policy, from Amos 3:7, requires Him to reveal His secrets to the prophets. In the seventh church age, a final messenger restores all mysteries hidden by ecclesiastical confusion, calling out the true Bride through the living Word made manifest.
  • The Bride as a Living Seed. As different seeds grow according to their nature (Luther brought forth Luther-seed, Pentecost brought Pentecost), the Bride is a living manifestation of God's eternal Word for this age—not mere organization or doctrine, but Christ Himself declared in the people by the Holy Spirit's power.
  • The Current Age: Seventh Church Age and Hour of Testing. We are in the seventh watch—Enoch's type—when lamp-trimming occurs and the Bridegroom is announced. The ecclesiastical system (denominational structures) is dead and will be burned; believers must come entirely into Christ, not into Methodist, Baptist, or even Pentecostal labels.
  • The Great Distinction: Wheat vs. Cocklebur. Though both respond to water (the Spirit) and both may shout, speak in tongues, or prophesy, only those predestinated as God's eternal seed will be gathered in the Rapture. The Bride ripens under God's election before the great Combine arrives.

Quote

"For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, and with the voice of the archangel, and the trump of God: and the dead in Christ shall rise first."

Brother Branham emphasizes that these three elements—Shout, Voice, Trump—must occur before Jesus Himself appears, drawing directly from 1 Thessalonians 4:16.

"God's Spirit, moving on. 'I the Lord have plant It. I'll water It, day and night. Lest some should—' He ordained these things to be, and He must send This."

Affirming God's sovereign plan: He plants His Word, waters it, and sends the final messenger to bring it to completion in the seventh age.

Final Exhortation

Believers are called to break free from organizational religion and denominational structures, coming entirely into Christ as living, manifested sons and daughters who bear the fruit of God's eternal Word for this hour. The Rapture is not merely a future event but a sifting judgment occurring now—separating the wheat (the predestinated Bride) from the chaff (those who follow false anointings and human tradition). Do not build your foundation on the changing policies of men but on the unchanging Word of God, and watch as He manifests Himself through a living people who refuse to return to the bondage of ecclesiastical systems.
