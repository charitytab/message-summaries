Leadership

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Friday, December 7, 1965
Location: Covina Bowl, Covina, California
Reference: 65-1207

Summary

True leadership and eternal life can only come through accepting Jesus Christ as Lord, not through human institutions, traditions, or self-effort; every believer must confront this ultimate choice and refuse false leadership that promises salvation through organizations rather than through Him.

Scripture Foundation

  • Mark 10:17-22 — the rich young ruler who came to Jesus asking about eternal life.
  • Matthew 15:8-9 — worship in vain through teaching commandments of men.
  • Hebrews 13:8 — Jesus Christ the same yesterday, today, and forever.

Also referenced: John 1:1,14 (the Word made flesh); Hebrews 11:23-29 (Moses by faith accepting Christ's reproach); Matthew 24:24 (deceiving the elect in the last days); Malachi 4 (the promise of the prophet in the last days).

Main Points

  • Leadership cascades through human influence. Every person is led by something: first a mother teaches you to walk, then a schoolteacher educates you, then a father instructs you in business, then a minister or priest claims authority over your spiritual life. The question is: who leads you ultimately?
  • The rich young ruler possessed all human excellences but lacked eternal life. He was intelligent, successful, religious, well-cultured, obedient to commandments from his youth, and Jesus even loved him—yet he did not possess eternal life. No amount of character, achievement, or religious observance can substitute for receiving the gift of eternal life.
  • Sincere worship performed outside God's provided way is worship in vain. Cain worshiped sincerely but was rejected because he did not follow God's prescribed channel. The Pharisees were religious but rejected Jesus. Following the right organization or ritual while rejecting God's living Word produces no effect; the Word must be received in its proper way for the age.
  • God has always revealed His will through prophets, not through democratic consensus or human consultation. When David wanted to bring the ark back to Jerusalem, he consulted all the captains and leaders, yet they failed to inquire of God's prophet Nathan. Consulting only human wisdom—no matter how sincere or well-intentioned—leads to messing up God's purpose and substituting human organization for God's order.
  • In this age, Christ is calling a Bride out of the church organizations, just as Moses called a nation out of a nation. The Bride age cannot be formed within denominational structures (Luther's justification, Wesley's sanctification, Pentecost's restoration of gifts are past phases). The true Church must accept the living Person of Jesus Christ—the Word—manifested for this age, not cling to the organizations and messages of former ages.

Quote

"We've got to channel ourselves into God's provided channel and God's way of doing it. No matter how much we think it's right, it's got to be according to the Word of the Lord or it's in vain."

Brother Branham emphasizes that sincere intent is insufficient; the work must be done God's way, in God's prescribed manner, or it produces no lasting effect.

"The only One that can give you Eternal Life. He's the—No matter how good you are, what you quit doing, what you start doing, you've got to accept the Person of the Lord Jesus Christ."

The minister stresses that personal virtue and religious behavior cannot earn or substitute for eternal life, which is a free gift dependent solely on accepting Christ as Leader and Savior.

"In the last days...the two would be so close it would deceive the very genes, predestinated, the Elected Ones, if it was possible."

Referring to Matthew 24:24, Brother Branham warns that in the final age, the counterfeit (the shuck of past denominations) will appear almost identical to the true (the Bride being formed), making discernment critical.

Final Exhortation

Do not trust in your church membership, your good works, your denomination's traditions, or your own sincerity to secure eternal life. Every hearer is confronted with a personal choice: reject the false leadership of human organization and accept the sole leadership of Jesus Christ, the living Word made flesh for this age. Denominational identity and past religious experiences have no bearing on your standing before God today; only acceptance of Christ's Person and submission to His Word will prepare you for the resurrection. This is an urgent hour—we are in the Bride age, the final phase before the harvest—and the only way forward is to come out from the religious shucks and tassels of former ages and be gathered into the Body of Christ.
