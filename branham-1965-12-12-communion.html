Communion

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, December 12, 1965 (evening)
Location: Tucson Tabernacle, Tucson, Arizona
Reference: 65-1212

Summary

Communion is not a spiritualized abstraction or a casual church tradition; it is a literal ordinance, instituted by Paul and binding upon the Church, which must be approached with sincerity, self-examination, and a life that actually reflects the Word one has received.

Scripture Foundation

  • 1 Corinthians 11:23–34 — Paul's institution of the Lord's Supper (read from verse 23; main preaching text).

Also referenced: Galatians 1:8; John 6:53 ("Except you eat the Flesh of the Son of man and drink His Blood, you have no Life in you"); Acts 2:38; Isaiah 28 ("Precept must be upon precept... all the tables of the Lord has become full of vomit").

Main Points

  • Three literal ordinances remain to the Church. Brother Branham names only three physical Divine orders: communion, feet-washing, and water baptism. All three are outward symbols of an inward work of grace and cannot be substituted by a purely "spiritual" observance.
  • Communion is an ordinance, not a transformation. The bread and cup do not become the literal body of Christ (he explicitly rejects the Catholic view), nor does water baptism itself forgive sins. Each is a command to be kept as a commemoration of what Christ has already done.
  • Sincerity is the dividing line. What God ordained as a sacrifice in the Old Testament became "a stench in His nose" once Israel took it as tradition. The same danger applies to communion today — taken unworthily, it brings damnation rather than blessing.
  • Self-examination is commanded. Paul's instruction to "tarry one for the other" means to pause, pray, and make right any offense with a brother before approaching the table. "If we judge ourself, we won't be judged."
  • The Message must be received personally, not secondhand. Brother Branham warns against taking communion from an intellectual or inherited standpoint. It is a revelation that one is part of Christ and part of His Body — a living union, not a religious habit.

Quote

"No. Anything that has a beginning has an end, but it's that which had not a beginning has no end."

Recounting his son Joseph's question about where God came from, Brother Branham answers that God, being Eternal, had no beginning and therefore has no end — framing Communion against the backdrop of the Eternal One the Church remembers.

"There was another William Branham... A little spot of Eternal Life down there, from the genes of God, the Word of God that was placed in there."

He recalls his boyhood on the creek bank, wondering how a flower could grow where he had built a fire the winter before — an illustration of the predestinated seed of Eternal Life that was in him long before his conversion.

Final Exhortation

Come to the Lord's table with reverence, love, and respect, knowing that by God's grace you have been saved. Examine yourself, make right any wrong with a brother, and take the bread and cup as a declaration that you believe every Word of God and live by it to the best of your knowledge. If Israel lived forty years in the wilderness without one feeble one among them by the type of this sacrifice, what will the Anti-type — the Body of Jesus Christ — do for His Bride who receives Him worthily?
