The Stone Which The Builders Refused Is Become The Head Of The Corner

Minister: Brother Joseph Coleman
Date Preached: Sunday, September 26, 1993
Location: Local Christian Assembly, Forest Hills, New York

Summary

The Stone rejected by human builders—Christ—has become the Headstone of the corner in the manifestation of the Bride through the Shekinah Glory, fulfilling the prophecy for the sealing and rapturing of believers in the last days.

Scripture Foundation

  • Isaiah 28:16 — "Behold, I lay in Zion for a foundation a stone, a tried stone, a precious corner stone, a sure foundation"
  • I Peter 2:4-5 — "To whom coming, as unto a living stone...Ye also, as lively stones, are built up a spiritual house"

Ephesians 2:19-22; Ephesians 4:13; I Peter 4:17; I Thessalonians 4:16

Main Points

  • The Foundation Stone Laid in Zion. Brother Coleman references Isaiah 28:16, which declares that God lays a tried stone, a precious cornerstone, and a sure foundation in Zion. He connects this to a 1985 revelation of "Charity, the Stone of Power," which became evident through Signs and Wonders in Wales and South Africa. Today's positive focus is on verse 16 alone, emphasizing that believers need not panic or run—they possess the correct Inspiration (the Seven Thunders) to understand Translation Faith.
  • Fellowship and Construction of God's House. Using Ephesians 2:19-22, Brother Coleman shows that believers are now citizens with the saints, no longer strangers. The Seed of Christ is "snowballing" and rolling like the Stone in Daniel's vision—unstoppable and growing without human hands. The Church is being built as a holy temple, with Jesus Christ Himself as the Cornerstone.
  • The Three-Part Rapture Sequence. Drawing on I Thessalonians 4:16 and citing Brother Branham's teaching, Brother Coleman emphasizes that three things must occur before the Lord's corporeal appearance: a Shout, the Voice of the Archangel, and the Trump of God, resulting in the dead in Christ rising first. The Rapture is not merely a word borrowed from tradition but the biblical reality of being "caught up."
  • The Headstone Manifestation and Holy Ghost Sealing. The sermon's climax reveals that the Stone the builders refused is now manifesting as the Headstone (Capstone)—not the Cornerstone. This is the sealing of the Bride with the Holy Ghost, where believers are sealed from their human spirit outward, barring unbelief from entering. The Shekinah Glory visibly came down in New York on the preceding Sunday, identifying the Headstone's presence.
  • Victory and Readiness for Rapturing Grace. The sermon concludes with the declaration that the identification is complete and believers are sealed unto salvation. Through the Rapturing Grace, the Bride moves from Virtue to Knowledge to Temperance to Patience to Godliness to Brotherly Kindness, culminating in the Capping Stone that magnetizes them for the Translation. The identification of the Headstone has taken place, and the Fire of God's Anointing rests upon the assembly.

Believers who recognize the Shekinah Glory's manifestation and the sealing of the Holy Ghost need not fear the trials outlined in Isaiah 28:17-22, knowing they possess the correct Inspiration (the Seven Thunders) and the Blood of Christ. The focus is not on judgment but on the joy of identification—the Bride is sealed, the Headstone is revealed, and the Church is ready for the Rapture. Let your faith be unshakable, knowing you stand in the purpose of God's last-day Word, sealed by the Magnetism of the Capping Stone itself.
