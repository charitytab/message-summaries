Anointed Seven Thunder Resurrection Faith

Minister: Brother Joseph Coleman
Date Preached: Sunday, April 19, 1981
Location: Forest Hills, New York
Reference: 1981-04-19

Summary

On Easter morning 1981, Brother Joseph Coleman proclaimed that the Bride has moved into the Dynamics period through anointed faith in the Seven Thunder Message. The Message centers on the resurrection power of Christ being present and operative through believers who have anchored their faith upon the Prophet's revelation and the Word. Brother Coleman emphasized that faith is not presumption or hope, but present-tense believing and speaking the promises of God in this hour. The believer's subconscious must be fixed and aligned with the Word, making them a channel of communication through which the inspiration of the Holy Spirit can flow. This Easter message declared that the season of the Bride's adoption and perfection has come through the anointing of their faith in the revealed Word for this day.

Main Points

  • The subconscious mind must be fixed with faith in God's promise. Every field of endeavor requires a fixed idea planted deep in the subconscious; when the Word lines up with this foundation, faith becomes immovable and sealed. The devil cannot shake what is locked inside, and this is how the Bride moves forward—with hearts fixed as David's was fixed to see the King.
  • The Word must dethrone human reasoning and imagination in the conscious mind. Goliath stands as a symbol of head knowledge and fleshly reasoning that opposes the Word. The Holy Spirit works to align the subconscious fixed idea with the conscious mind, bypassing the doubt that the devil continually stirs up through intellect and emotion.
  • The Bride has entered the period of Dynamics through anointed Seven Thunder faith. After years of labor contending for the faith, the Holy Spirit has now anointed the fixed idea within believers, transforming invisible faith into visible resurrection power. This is not hope or presumption but present-tense reality—"NOW faith is"—in the atmosphere.
  • Faith is a fight and a labor that requires speaking and declaring God's Word despite all opposition. Faith is not passive belief; it is active declaration. Believers must deliberately and purposefully return to God's promise, speak it into reality, and stand against every demon and circumstance that contradicts it. Seven Thunders give faith for rapturing grace, and this must be spoken into being.
  • Believers are channels of communication through which God's inspiration flows for this day. When the Word is in the Bride and the Bride abides in the Word, she becomes a conduit for God's inspiration to heal, deliver, and work miracles. The Prophet was the channel for his day; now the Bride—clothed in the written Word and the quickening Spirit—carries that anointing forward.
  • The resurrection faith of this hour breaks the Bride free from all natural limitations and worldly identity. Through anointed faith, the believer is made whole, cast free from complexes, attitudes, and national or racial spirits. The makeup and personality of the Bride becomes Love, Peace, Joy, Temperance, and Patience—reflecting the Father's nature—and she walks as a living sign of the resurrection.

Once the Word is sealed in the deepest part of a believer's being, neither doubt nor the devil can dislodge it. The Bride stands as an unshakeable people, identified for this day, in resurrection power.

Final Exhortation

Brother Coleman closed by exhorting believers to speak and declare the promises of God for this hour without hesitation or explanation. He called on Fort Wayne and the churches everywhere to rise up as channels of communication through which the Holy Spirit inspires and empowers the work of God. The message concluded with a charge: go tell the disciples that God is not dead, but alive and moving in the midst of His people with signs, wonders, and the outpouring of the Holy Ghost. The Bride is to walk in rapturing grace, filled with resurrection faith, proclaiming to a dying world that Christ is risen and His power is present in this day.
