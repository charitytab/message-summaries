The Trial

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, April 19, 1964
Location: Tampa, Florida
Reference: 64-0419

Summary

Brother William Branham presents a courtroom drama in which God's Word stands on trial against the accusations of a skeptical world. Satan serves as prosecuting attorney, calling three witnesses — Mr. Unbeliever, Mr. Skeptic, and Mr. Impatience — each testifying that God's promises do not hold. Against these accusations, Brother Branham calls the Holy Ghost as the Defense Witness — presenting the irrefutable evidence of lives transformed, bodies healed, and the Word demonstrated in power. The congregation is seated as jury, called to weigh the evidence and render a verdict on the faithfulness of God.

Scripture Foundation

  • Mark 16:9-20 - Jesus' post-resurrection appearances and the promise of signs following believers (central to the defense)
  • Mark 11:22-24 - "Have faith in God...whosoever shall say unto this mountain, Be thou removed...he shall have whatsoever he saith" (testimony of Mr. Impatience)
  • James 5:14-15 - "Is any sick among you? let him call for the elders of the church...the prayer of faith shall save the sick" (testimony of Mr. Skeptic)

Also referenced: The authenticity of Mark 16 in certain manuscripts; testimony of a mother healed through simple faith in the Word

Main Points

  • God's Word Is Placed on Trial by Every Generation. The world's prosecution rests on the claim that God's promises fail. This is not a new charge; it is the oldest accusation Satan has leveled against God's character.
  • Three Witnesses Represent Three Categories of Failed Faith. Unbelief, skepticism, and impatience are not evidence against God — they are evidence of the conditions under which the promises were sought.
  • The Holy Ghost Is the Defense Witness. Where human testimony fails, the Spirit testifies through demonstrated power — healing, transformation, and revealed knowledge that no natural mind can explain.
  • The Congregation Serves as Jury. Brother Branham positions every listener as a judge called to weigh evidence and render a verdict. To side with the prosecution is to call God a liar.
  • Authentic Faith Produces Results — on God's Timetable. The failures presented by each witness stemmed not from God's inability but from conditions of unbelief, doubt, or impatience that short-circuited the working of faith.

Quote

"If God could heal me with that part of the Word that's not inspired, what would He do with that that really is inspired?"

This statement — from a woman healed through simple faith in a disputed passage — is the Defense's most powerful exhibit. Brother Branham uses it to challenge academic skepticism: if the healing power of God operates even through a passage some scholars question, then the authenticity of His Word rests not on scholarly consensus but on its demonstrated power in the lives of those who believe it.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham urges the congregation to render a faithful verdict: the evidence is in, and God's Word is not guilty. Believers must refuse to side with the prosecution of Unbelief, Skepticism, or Impatience, and instead stand firm on the conviction that God keeps every promise He has ever made. The trial is won not through human argument but through faith in the living God and His unbroken record of faithfulness.
