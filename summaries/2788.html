Summary

Brother William Branham traces the providential story of his life before the Los Angeles congregation, sharing how God's hand guided him from humble beginnings through repeated supernatural encounters to a worldwide healing ministry. The message is not merely personal biography but a testimony to God's faithfulness — demonstrating through Brother Branham's own experience that God still calls, commissions, and equips servants to do the works of Christ, giving the congregation faith that the same God who worked in his life will work in theirs.

Quote

"God, in His universe, God keeping His Word — the works that I do, shall you also."

Brother Branham anchors his entire life testimony in this promise from John 14:12. His story is not presented as unique to him alone but as evidence of what God will do for any yielded believer. The same God who worked through him will work through all who believe and surrender to His Word.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham calls the congregation to stand in reverent, active faith in God's Word. His life story is offered not for personal glory but as a testimony to stir faith in every listener: if God could take a poor, uneducated boy from the hills of Kentucky and use him to demonstrate the power of John 14:12 around the world, there is nothing He cannot do for anyone who believes and yields to Him.
