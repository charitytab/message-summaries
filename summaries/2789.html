Show Us The Father And It Will Satisfy Us

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, April 19, 1959 (evening service)
Location: Los Angeles, California
Reference: 59-0419E

Summary

Brother Branham opens this evening service at the Angelus Temple by addressing the fundamental human yearning for a revelation of God the Father. Using Philip's request in John 14 as his foundation, he teaches that God manifests Himself in four dimensions — in His universe, in His Word, in His Son Jesus Christ, and in His people — and that the evidence of His presence is seen most clearly through the testimonies of those who have been saved, healed, and transformed. The message calls the congregation and radio audience to recognize God's active presence among them and to come with faith, knowing that the same Father who satisfied Philip's longing still satisfies the seeking heart today.

Scripture Foundation

  • John 14:7-8 - "If ye had known me, ye should have known my Father also...Philip saith unto him, Lord, shew us the Father, and it sufficeth us" (sermon foundation)
  • John 10:38 - "Believe the works; that ye may know, and believe, that the Father is in me, and I in him" (God revealed through His works)
  • Isaiah 53:5 - "He was wounded for our transgressions; with his stripes we are healed" (basis for healing in prayer line)
  • John 5:24 - "He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life" (salvation promise)
  • James 5:14-15 - The prayer of faith shall save the sick (basis for anointing prayer)

Also referenced: Romans 10:17 (faith by hearing), Psalm 23, Hebrews 13:8 (Jesus Christ the same), Luke 11:27-28, Matthew 28:19-20

Main Points

  • God Manifests Himself in Four Ways. Brother Branham teaches that God reveals Himself in His universe (creation), in His Word (Scripture), in His Son (Jesus Christ), and in His people (the church). Each manifestation provides cumulative evidence of His reality and active presence.
  • The Evidence of God's Presence Is His People. When thousands raise their hands testifying that they have passed from death unto life, when the sick are healed, and when those in despair find joy and peace, these are demonstrations that God is actively working through His church.
  • Salvation, Healing, and Transformation Satisfy the Longing Heart. The heart's deep yearning for God is not met by argument or philosophy but by encounter — by seeing God work in the lives of real people around you.
  • The Prayer Line Is a Demonstration of God's Presence. When the Holy Spirit reveals knowledge about individuals' conditions through the gift of discernment, it confirms that God is present and fulfilling His Word.
  • Faith and Confession Are Essential to Receiving. Believers must confess their healing and believe, not doubting. God makes the body obey the confession of faith.

Quote

"God is in His universe, God is in His Word, God is in His Son Jesus Christ, and God is in His people. Have you seen Him in His universe? Have you seen Him in His Word? Have you seen Him in His Son? Have you seen Him in His people? Sure, now He's here."

In this statement, Brother Branham crystallizes the evening's message: God is not distant or abstract but actively present and discernible through multiple avenues of revelation. The progression from creation to Scripture to Christ to His people builds an overwhelming testimony to God's reality.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham calls all who have raised their hands in faith to receive what they have confessed. He urges believers of all denominations to stand united in the confidence that God will fulfill His promise: "The prayer of faith shall save the sick; God shall raise them up." Do not doubt; rise confessing your healing, because the Father who satisfied Philip is the same Father present tonight, and He keeps His Word.
