The Perfection

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Friday, April 19, 1957
Location: Jeffersonville, Indiana
Reference: 57-0419

Summary

God requires absolute perfection from all who stand in His presence, yet no fallen human being can achieve this through their own effort — only through the perfect sacrifice of Jesus Christ. Brother Branham teaches that while the Old Testament law revealed humanity's sinfulness and could never perfect anyone through animal sacrifice, the one offering of Christ on Calvary has eternally perfected all believers through His blood, making them blameless before God if they come to Him by faith, confess their sins, and receive His Spirit.

Scripture Foundation

  • Matthew 5:47-48 - "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect" (Christ's command establishing the standard)
  • Hebrews 10:1, 11-12 - The law had a shadow of good things to come, but "by one offering he has perfected for ever them that are sanctified" (contrast of law and grace)
  • Romans 8:1 - "There is therefore now no condemnation to them that are in Christ Jesus" (assurance of standing through the blood)
  • I Corinthians 12:13 - Baptism by one Spirit into the Body of Christ (how perfection is received)

Also referenced: Genesis (garden of Eden, the fall), Isaiah 53:5 (healing through His stripes), Job's declaration of faith, the Old Testament sacrificial system as type

Main Points

  • The Law Could Never Perfect Anyone. The law was only a shadow pointing to the perfect sacrifice — it condemned but could not cure, showed sin but provided no remedy. Every annual sacrifice left the worshipper with the same desire to sin still present within.
  • Only a Perfect, Sinless Sacrifice Could Satisfy Divine Justice. No human could atone for another since all are born in sin. God required an innocent victim — first symbolized through lambs, but ultimately fulfilled in Jesus Christ, whose blood came not through sexual generation but through divine creation.
  • Christ's Blood Has Already Paid the Sin Penalty Forever. When a believer by faith applies the blood of Jesus and the Holy Ghost comes to sanctify them, that person stands perfected and blameless before God — not by human effort but by the sovereign grace of God through Christ's vicarious suffering.
  • True Perfection Is a Confession of Unworthiness Plus God's Grace. Perfection means admitting you cannot save yourself, surrendering to Jesus as personal Saviour, and receiving the Spirit's baptism into the Body of Christ — after which you have no more desire for sin because the Holy Ghost has taken residence.
  • Come as You Are — Do Not Wait to Reform Yourself. No amount of personal righteousness, church joining, ceremonies, or works will merit anything in God's sight. Come broken and confessing; let Christ be your all-sufficient remedy.

Quote

"By one offering he has perfected for ever them that are sanctified."

This statement from Hebrews 10:12 is the key to Calvary's meaning. Brother Branham emphasizes that Christ's sacrifice was not repeated year after year like the Old Testament offerings — it was a single, eternally sufficient act that accomplished what the law could never do: it made believers forever perfect in the presence of God Almighty. The believer who comes by faith and confesses their sins stands justified and perfected not because of personal merit, but because the blood of Jesus has already settled the sin question forever.

Final Exhortation

On this Good Friday evening, Brother Branham calls sinners to come to Calvary by faith, confess all sins, and accept Jesus as personal Saviour. He urges listeners not to delay, thinking they must first clean themselves up or conquer their sins on their own — that will never work. Instead, come just as you are to the cross, acknowledge your guilt, place your faith in the perfect sacrifice of Christ, and let His blood cover you. Only then will the Holy Spirit come and remove the very desire to sin, and you will stand perfected forever in the presence of God.
