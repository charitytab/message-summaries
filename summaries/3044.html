Earnestly Contending For The Faith That Was Once Delivered Unto The Saints

Minister: Brother William Branham
Date Preached: Sunday, April 19, 1956
Location: Spindale, North Carolina
Reference: 56-0419

Summary

Brother Branham emphasizes the critical importance of returning to the original faith once delivered to the saints, as taught in Scripture and demonstrated by early believers. He contrasts the pristine power and supernatural manifestations of apostolic faith with modern denominational divisions, calling the Church to earnestly contend for authentic biblical truth rather than organizational traditions. The sermon establishes that divine healing flows from God's completed work in Christ, and that faith—not the minister's personal gift—is the foundation for receiving healing and experiencing the supernatural power that characterized the early Church.

Scripture Foundation

  • Jude 1:3 - "Earnestly contend for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints" (foundation of sermon title and central call)
  • Ephesians 4:30 - Reference to the Holy Spirit and spiritual sealing, undergirding the call to preserve the original faith

Also referenced: Romans, I Corinthians, and other epistles concerning faith, healing, and the work of the Holy Spirit; the apostolic Church as model

Main Points

  • The Faith Once Delivered Must Be Recovered. The apostolic Church possessed a clear, biblically-defined faith characterized by supernatural power, divine healing, and the active operation of the Holy Spirit. Modern Christianity must examine whether it has retained or abandoned these essential elements.
  • Divine Healing Is God's Work, Not the Minister's Gift. Preaching divine healing does not make the minister a healer; healing comes through God's completed work. Just as preaching salvation is based on Christ's atonement, divine healing flows from God's eternal provision—the minister is only a vessel.
  • Sin and Sickness Are Inseparable. One cannot address sin without addressing sickness, because sickness is an attribute of sin. Disobedience brought sickness into the human race, and healing is part of God's redemptive work through the blood of Christ.
  • Proper Approach Is Essential. Just as one must approach earthly dignitaries and judges with appropriate protocol, believers must approach divine healing through right understanding of Scripture, proper faith, and recognition of God's sovereignty.
  • Denominational Division Obscures Original Truth. With over nine hundred different denominational churches each claiming to contend for the faith, believers must return to Scripture alone to discover what the original apostolic faith actually was and compare it with modern teaching.

Quote

"You cannot deal with sin in any manner without dealing with sickness. You have to deal with it because it's an attribute of sin. Sickness come from sin. Disobedience brought sickness."

Brother Branham teaches that healing cannot be separated from redemption. Since sickness entered the world through disobedience and is rooted in sin, the same divine work that addresses sin must address sickness. This unified view of healing and salvation corrects modern fragmentary approaches that attempt to deal with spiritual matters while ignoring the body's restoration.

Final Exhortation

Brother Branham calls the Church to earnestly contend for the authentic faith of the apostles by returning to Scripture as the sole authority. In a landscape of religious confusion and denominational multiplicity, believers must seek the power and presence of the Holy Spirit that characterized the early Church, embracing divine healing as God's provided way and demonstrating supernatural faith rather than organizational loyalty. The opportunity to receive healing and experience God's power is available to all who approach Him through faith in His Word, regardless of the minister's personal identity or gift.
